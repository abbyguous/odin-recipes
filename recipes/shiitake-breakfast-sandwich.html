Shiitake Breakfast Sandwich

Some buttery umami goodness to greet the day.

Once saw an IG reel about a sandwich like this and then couldn't for the life of me find it again. Lost to the ravages of time. So let's improvise with what we have in the fridge.

This bright, buttery, cheesy, umami sandwich pairs really well with honey citron tea and extra cherry tomatoes on the side.

Prep: 15 mins    Cook: 20 mins    Servings: 2


Ingredients

  • 4 slices whole wheat bread with the crusts removed
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 eggs
  • 6 pieces dried shiitake
  • 6 cherry tomatoes
  • Grated parmesan
  • Olive oil
  • Kewpie mayo
  • Sriracha
  • Salt
  • Black pepper

Steps

  1. In a bowl, rehydrate the shiitake with boiled water.
  2. Meanwhile, slice your cherry tomatoes, and beat each egg in a bowl of its own and mix in some Kewpie mayo.
  3. When the shiitake are soft enough, slice them thinly and sauté on high heat in olive oil. Season lightly with salt and pepper. Set aside.
  4. Melt some butter in the pan on low heat. Toast the slices of whole wheat bread until almost slightly burnt on both sides. Set aside.
  5. Coat the pan with olive oil. Pour in one beaten egg, and place half of the shiitake all over the egg. When the shiitake omelette is three-fourths cooked, put two slices of bread on it side by side. Make sure the omelette's cooked before you flip it along with the slices of bread.
  6. On the flipside, layer some cherry tomatoes, sprinkle some grated parmesan, and drizzle enough sriracha to wake you up.
  7. Fold the sandwich. Repeat for the other serving. Enjoy!
