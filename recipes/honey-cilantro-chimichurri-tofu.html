Honey Cilantro Chimichurri Tofu

If one has the aldehyde-sensitive variation of the OR6A2 gene, proceed with caution.

Cilantro isn't always available at the closest supermarket, so whenever it shows up, best believe I'm making stuff with cilantro in it for at least 3 days.

The secret to evenly cooked tofu is patting it dry with paper towels.

Prep: 15 mins    Cook: 15 mins    Servings: 2


Ingredients

  • 380g tokwa (firm tofu)
  • A handful of cilantro
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • Soy sauce
  • Honey
  • Sukang pinakurat (spiced coconut vinegar)
  • Olive oil

Steps

  1. Slice your tofu into Spam-shaped pieces. Take a paper towel, fold it crosswise, cover your left hand with one end, put a piece of tofu on it, and press the other end of the towel on top of the tofu with your right hand. Don't press too hard or the tofu will crumble. Repeat for the rest of the tofu.
  2. Preheat your pan well, pour in some olive oil, and place the tofu carefully so they're all evenly sitting on the pan. Fry on medium high heat until golden brown on both sides.
  3. While the tofu's cooking, wash your cilantro and roughly chop it up. Chop up your garlic as well.
  4. Mix soy sauce, honey, vinegar, garlic, cilantro, and olive oil in a bowl.
  5. Plate your tofu and drizzle it with the chimichurri. Best eaten with rice. Manihapon ta!
